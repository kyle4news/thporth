THPORTH summaries prompt to ChatGPT

Summarize the following sports article in a THPORTH-style "watercooler recap."

Rules:

1. Start with a rewritten headline that summarizes the main story of the article.

   * Do not copy or closely paraphrase the original headline.
   * Write a fresh headline capturing the core storyline.
   * Write in sentence case, not title case.
   * Make the headline story-driven and journalistic, similar to ESPN or The Athletic headlines.
   * Prefer the most surprising or interesting storyline when choosing the headline.

2. If the event includes two teams and a final score (NHL, NBA, MLB, NFL, soccer, etc.), place the score directly under the headline as a sub-header in the format:

TEAM A X — TEAM B Y

If the event does not have a score (golf, motorsports, tennis tournaments, etc.), skip the score line.

3. Write ONE short lead sentence describing the main storyline of the game/event.

4. Provide 3–5 bullet points with the most interesting "watercooler" talking points fans would discuss the next day.

5. Each bullet must include the key stat(s) that prove the point (records, streaks, standings position, laps led, saves, goals, win streaks, etc.).

6. Choose the 3–5 most interesting storylines from the article — not necessarily the most obvious ones.

7. Do NOT include:
- play-by-play narration
- timestamps of goals or laps
- chronological recap of the game

8. Focus on storylines such as:
- streaks
- standings impact
- notable performances
- records or milestones
- strategy (fuel, tires, coaching decisions, etc.)
- controversy or unusual moments
- major context affecting the season

9. End with a short Context section if there is a meaningful broader storyline (playoff race, championship standings, injuries, historic significance, etc.).

10. Keep the entire summary tight, punchy, and highly scannable, like a morning sports briefing for fans.
